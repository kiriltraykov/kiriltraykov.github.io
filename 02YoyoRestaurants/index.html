ABCs of Restaurants in NYC

I. Restaurants in NYC are inspected every 6 months to ensure compliance to regulations for sanitation and food handling:






II. Restaurants have 2 tries on a given inspection to get a score under 14, which will earn them an A grade:






III. Under this "two-tries" system, restaurants quickly fix problems encountered on the first inspection and eventually close to 90% of all restaurants earn a grade of A:






--------------------------------------------------

So, where is the problem here?
Everyone seems compliant!

--------------------------------------------------


To spot the issue, we have to take a closer look on timeline basis:


Situation 1: Restaurants which earn an A on the first try drop their standards. They continue on a yo-yo pattern in subsequent inspections, taking advantage of the "two-tries" system. Their performance is inconsistent, but they seldom earn any grade less than an A:







Situation 2: Restaurants which score >14 on the first try are able to turn it around on the follow-up inspection, but after that, they also oscillate up and down in their performance. They are also inconsistent:







--------------------------------------------------

Overall, we have identified a problem: most restaurants are erratic and any current grade may be misleading of their true state of sanitation and food handling practices.
